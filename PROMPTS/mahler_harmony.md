## Harmonic Vocabulary

These are the primary chords of this idiom, listed in order of tension:

| Chord | Symbol | Function | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| F major | III | Primary resting point | Warm, open, luminous |
| F major 7th | Fmaj7 | Enriched III | Glowing, slightly suspended |
| B half-diminished 7th | Bø7 | Chromatic pivot / tension | Yearning, unstable, signature sound |
| C9sus4 / Gm7/C | V sus | Dominant preparation | Floating, unresolved |
| Dm7 | im7 | True tonic (use sparingly) | Tender, fragile |
| B♭maj7 | ♭VImaj7 | Plagal warmth | Dark richness, quasi-resolution |
| A7 | V7 | Dominant (rare, powerful) | Propulsive, declarative |
| Eø7 | iim7♭5 | Supertonic half-diminished | Dark preparation |
| C#°7 | vii°7 | Leading-tone fully diminished | Maximum tension, peak |

### The Signature Sound: Bø7
**B half-diminished 7th (B–D–F–A)** is the harmonic fingerprint of this style.
- In early phrases: voice it **over an F bass** (Bø7/F) — cushioned, ambiguous.
- In later phrases: voice it in **root position** (B in the bass) — raw, intense.
- The move from Bø7/F to Bø7 (root) across phrases is one of the primary
  structural intensifications.

When prompting GenAI to compose in this style, use:

> Delay the C# (raised 7th of D harmonic minor) until the emotional climax.
> Never resolve to D minor with an authentic cadence — end instead on F major
> or a B♭ major chord. Harmonic rhythm should be 8–12 beats per chord. All voices
> should sustain long tones with staggered entries (blooming texture). Dynamic

> String Quartet No. 8."*
